About

I am a professor at Foundation Baptist College and the Church Administrator at Meadowlands Baptist Church in Edmonton, AB. In the college, I teach Bible exposition and church history courses, and in my day-to-day work at the church I do a lot of different things. I also have some computer science background from my undergrad education, so I’ve put together a number of online projects.

Personal

My wife Meg teaches private violin lessons, when she isn’t busy taking care of our daughter. Some of the things we like to do when we spend time together include reading, spending time with friends, and walking down to the neighborhood park with our little girl.

Professional

Teaching Experience

Courses I’ve taught at Foundation Baptist College include:

  • Su201 Life of Christ. A survey of the life of Christ as recorded in the four canonical gospels, with an emphasis on the application of a traditional grammatical-historical hermeneutic to the biblical text.
  • Pr103 Personal Evangelism. Establishes the believer’s obligation to evangelize. Demonstrates both the theology and methodologies for carrying out this obligation.
  • Ex201 Exposition of John’s Gospel. Analyzes major theological themes and exposits select portions in John. Highlights hermeneutical principles vital to a proper exposition of John’s writings.
  • Ex204 Exposition of Isaiah. An exposition of the book of Isaiah emphasizing the book’s unified structure and thematic development. Includes discussion of how to structure a preaching series from Isaiah and vital hermeneutical principles within the book.]
  • Ex402 Exposition of Mark. An exposition of the gospel of Mark. Emphasizes its role in the synoptic gospels and its theme. Discusses how to interpret New Testament narrative as well as parabolic literature and discourse.

Library Subject Guides

I published these research guides during my time as a research assistant at the Bob Jones University Mack Library. They contain valuable bibliographic and reference material for a variety of different topic areas. Other people are maintaining these now, but I did the vast majority of the work in producing these sites.

  • Biblical Commentaries (Technical). Lists technical, conservative commentaries for the books of the Bible.
  • Biblical Exegesis. Software and online resources for studying the biblical text.
  • Biblical Greek. Resources for Greek students at all levels.
  • Biblical Hebrew. Resources for Hebrew (and Aramaic) students at all levels.
  • Children’s Ministry Resources. A comprehensive list of outreach ministry resources (such as Sunday School materials, flannelgraph, puppets, etc) available for checkout, including photos.
  • New Testament Greco-Roman Backgrounds. Introductory material on the Greco-Roman world, including its history, society, culture, religion, and literature, in relation to the expansion of early Christianity and the New Testament documents.
  • Rare Bibles at Mack Library Lists rare Bibles stored in the Library’s Jerusalem Chamber and Special Collections.
  • Seminary Project Labs - Greek & Hebrew fonts, Turabian, Zotero. Guides users through the installation and configuration of Greek and Hebrew fonts, the use of the BJU Seminary Turabian wizard, and Zotero.
  • Sermon Index. Indexes sermons in books at the Mack Library by preacher and Scripture passage. Includes call numbers and page references.
